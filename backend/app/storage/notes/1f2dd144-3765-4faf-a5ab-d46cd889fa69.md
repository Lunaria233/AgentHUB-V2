### Task Summary: AI Agent Security, Governance & Compliance Trends (2024–2025)

**Executive Conclusions**
Current trends indicate that securing AI agents requires a fundamental shift from traditional perimeter security to **identity-first, zero-trust architectures**, as a single compromised agent can exfiltrate terabytes of data or manipulate business processes undetected (Obsidian Security). Governance is evolving to prioritize **complete decision traceability**, where audit trails must record every input, tool invocation, and reasoning step to satisfy regulatory requirements (Galileo AI). Compliance efforts are consolidating around established frameworks such as **NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001, and the EU AI Act**, necessitating cross-functional collaboration between security, legal, and engineering teams (Wiz; AccuKnox).

**Key Findings & Evidence**

*   **Security Risks & Mitigation:**
    *   **Threat Landscape:** Agents face specific risks including prompt injection, jailbreak attempts, bias exploitation, and unauthorized data access (AccuKnox). A compromised agent poses higher risk than traditional applications due to autonomous action capabilities (Obsidian Security).
    *   **Architectural Controls:** Effective protection requires workload identity providers, policy engines (e.g., OPA), real-time behavioral analytics, and network egress allowlisting (Obsidian Security).
    *   **Runtime Protection:** Solutions now include prompt firewalls to inspect inputs, runtime monitoring for abnormal behavior, and automated red teaming to test for vulnerabilities (AccuKnox).

*   **Governance & Audit Requirements:**
    *   **Decision Lineage:** Unlike traditional logs, agent-specific audit trails must preserve chronological records of decision-making logic to enable accountability and debugging (Galileo AI).

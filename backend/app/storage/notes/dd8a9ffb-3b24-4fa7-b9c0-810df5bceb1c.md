- Context Engineering for AI Agents: Lessons from the Trenches: # Context Engineering for AI Agents: Lessons from the Trenches | by Samuel Grummons | Medium. I’ve spent the last few months building AI agents that needed to be deeply personal — agents that could remember your preferences, understand your context, and respond as if they’d been working with you for months. Here’s what really broke my original approach: I needed some context to be cached for cost reasons (the foundation that rarely changes), while other context needed to be fresh every conversation turn (the user’s current state, recent activity, temporal information). Every conversation starts by loading the user profile via a “silent” tool call — it happens automatically, not at the AI’s discretion. Now the AI can make intelligent decisions about what context to refresh based on the conversation topic and data age. **Design principle**: Make context loading deterministic (same information architecture every conversation), but let AI decisions remain flexible (what additional data to retrieve based on conversation needs).

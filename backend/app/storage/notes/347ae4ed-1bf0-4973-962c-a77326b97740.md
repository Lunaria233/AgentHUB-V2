### Task Summary: Leading AI Agent Platforms Landscape (2024-2025)

**Key Conclusions**
There is no single dominant "universal" AI agent platform; market leadership is segmented by use case, technical expertise, and existing ecosystem integration. **Microsoft (AutoGen/Copilot Studio)** and **LangChain** are identified as primary leaders for development frameworks, while **Salesforce (Agentforce)** and **Google (Vertex AI/Agentspace)** lead in enterprise infrastructure. For SMBs and marketers, low-code automation tools like **Zapier** and **n8n** offer the highest accessibility [Snippet 1][Snippet 3][Snippet 4].

**Vendor Landscape & Value Propositions**
*   **Enterprise & Integration Leaders:** Microsoft AutoGen and Copilot Studio rank highly for organizations already using Microsoft technologies (SharePoint, Teams, Excel) due to seamless workflow integration [Snippet 1][Snippet 3]. Salesforce Agentforce scored 8.7/10 in business comparisons, optimized for CRM contexts, while Google Agentspace and Vertex AI also rank highly for enterprise needs [Snippet 1][Snippet 3].
*   **Development Frameworks:** LangChain remains a key open-source option for businesses requiring flexibility and complex multi-agent orchestration, though it demands higher technical expertise than turnkey solutions [Snippet 3].
*   **Customer Service & Conversational AI:** Specialized platforms dominate this vertical. Salesforce Agentforce (8.7/10), Intercom Fin AI (8.4/10), and Zendesk AI Agent (8.2/10) are prioritized for human agent efficiency and pre/post-sale workflows [Snippet 1]. Voiceflow is noted for conversational AI but may be restrictive for complex multi-agent systems [Snippet 3].
*   **Low-Code & Marketing Automation:** For marketers and smaller teams, visual node-based builders are preferred. **n8n** ($24/month) and **Zapier Agents** (free tier) are recommended

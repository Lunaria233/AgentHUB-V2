- Why 'Context Engineering' is the New Frontier for AI Agents - Vellum AI: # Why ‘Context Engineering’ is the New Frontier for AI Agents. The debate kicked off when Cognition, the company behind the AI coding agent Devin, posted a blog arguing that multi-agent architectures are a "tempting idea" that is "quite bad in practice." They advocate instead for a single-agent approach powered by what they call “Context Engineering.”. To understand why Context Engineering is causing a stir, we first need to understand the dominant paradigm for building complex agents: the multi-agent system. If prompt engineering is the art of crafting the perfect initial instruction, Context Engineering is the discipline of dynamically managing the entirety of information an agent sees at every step of a complex task. Even a single AI agent needs to retrieve information (a form of context), and a multi-agent system is useless if the individual agents have poor context. A top-level "Context Engineer" agent will manage the overall task. Crucially, each of these temporary agents will itself benefit from meticulously engineered context.
- Context Engineering for AI Agents: Lessons from the Trenches: # Context Engineering for AI Agents: Lessons from the Trenches | by Samuel Grummons | Medium. I’ve spent the last few months building AI agents that needed to be deeply personal — agents that could remember your preferences, understand your context, and respond as if they’d been working with you for months. Here’s what really broke my original approach: I needed some context to be cached for cost reasons (the foundation that rarely changes), while other context needed to be fresh every conversation turn (the user’s current state, recent activity, temporal information). Every conversation starts by loading the user profile via a “silent” tool call — it happens automatically, not at the AI’s discretion. Now the AI can make intelligent decisions about what context to refresh based on the conversation topic and data age. **Design principle**: Make context loading deterministic (same information architecture every conversation), but let AI decisions remain flexible (what additional data to retrieve based on conversation needs).
